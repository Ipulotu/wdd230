Beaumont Sport and Recreation Centre

The Beaumont Sport and Recreation Centre (BSRC) is a multi-use facility located in the heart of Beaumont. Featuring a NHL sized arena, hard court gymnasium, turf field house, aquatics centre, two-lane walking track, various multi-purpose spaces, The Arch Climbing Centre and a fitness centre expansion sponsored by Apple Fitness a divison of Liv North.

Coloniale Golf Club

Coloniale Golf Club is an 18-hole Championship links style golf course located just minutes south of Edmonton in the Town of Beaumont. Opened in 1993, our mission has always been to achieve the highest standard of course conditioning and customer service. In recent years we have regularly received a "4 Star Rating" from Golf Digest's Places to Play.

Clareview & Belmont Dental

Our VISION is to customize treatment to each patient individually and look after their dental health over the course of a lifetime. We will provide a safe, friendly environment to listen to your concerns, educate you and help you make the best dental treatment plan that is right for your goals.

Frequency Hearing

Frequency Hearing is a full-service adult hearing clinic in Beaumont, Alberta. Our small team of hearing health professionals are dedicated to helping you understand your hearing and the best options to manage hearing loss.

Insight Eye Care

Insight Eye Care is a full scope ocular clinic dedicated to providing patients with comprehensive and quality Eye Care and Designer Eyewear, to all age groups. We take care of ocular emergencies, as well as scheduled appointments. Our Optometrist, Dr. Amrita Mahal is well experienced and strives to offer the highest level of care, and for that, we are open late in the evening and on Saturday. We work with most insurance providers and ensure you get the best of care. We carry designer frames and sunglasses.

Jeb's Nofrills

The original No Frills store was just that, no frills. No product advertising, no store displays, no meat counter, no clerks to bag your groceries, and you had to bring your own bags or pay 3 cents for each. "It doesn't bother me a bit," replied first day customer Frank Atlas when asked about bagging his own groceries. "I've saved a couple dollars with this order."
